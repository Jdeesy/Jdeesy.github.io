What is Pair Programming?

Pair programming or peer-pairing is where you and another programmer work together to program a project. This allows a collaboration of idea and to help one another when stuck. Usually during these sessions one person will be the driver and one will be the navigator, which I'll talk about in a little bit.

Why is Pair Programming important to DBC?

Pair programming is important to DBC because in many cases you will be working with multiple people during a project. This will be good practice for the future. Not only that but this is another way to learn and teach programming.

Compare Driver and Navigator

Driver and navigator are the two roles each programmer uses during the pair programming. How the roles are defined are different for each person. For me, I define a driver as the person at the keyboard. They are the one entering in the code. The navigator is the the person that watches. They are the one making sure we stay on track, correct any error, and gives directions if needed.
